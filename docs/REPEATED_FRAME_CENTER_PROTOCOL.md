# Repeated-Frame Center Enumeration Protocol

## Status

This is a finite, outcome-aware extension of the named-frame audit. The first completed scan showed that both

- `Yeshua = 282_12`, framed by `YHWH = 22_12`; and
- `Yeshua HaMashiach = 525_12`, framed by `Adonai = 55_12`

have a center digit equal to their conventional base-10 digital root and base-12 digits summing to 12.

Those two cases were known before this extension was frozen. The extension asks whether any other three-digit base-12 palindrome with a repeated outer digit satisfies the same two conditions. It is a complete enumeration, not an independent prediction or probability calculation.

## Fixed Universe

Enumerate every base-12 numeral of the form

`ada_12`

where:

- the repeated outer digit `a` ranges from `1` through `B`;
- the center digit `d` ranges from `0` through `B`.

There are exactly `11 x 12 = 132` members. Leading zero is excluded because the universe is restricted to three-digit numerals.

## Recorded Properties

For every member, record:

- outer digit and center digit;
- base-12 numeral and decimal value;
- conventional base-10 digital root;
- numerical sum of the three base-12 digits;
- whether the center equals the decimal digital root;
- whether the base-12 digit sum equals 12;
- whether both conditions hold.

## Corpus Contact

After the arithmetic enumeration is complete, exact standard-gematria and Mispar Gadol values from `data/master_results.csv` and `data/divine_name_expansion_results.csv` may be joined to the 132 rows. Every name must retain its source layer and value-system label.

The output will distinguish:

- arithmetic solutions in the complete 132-member universe;
- solutions occupied by admitted expressions;
- named outer frames supplied by another admitted expression.

## Interpretation Limit

The enumeration can prove how many members satisfy the two equations within the declared universe. It cannot establish why Yeshua or Yeshua HaMashiach occupies a solution, whether the numerical forms were intended, or how much theological weight the correspondence should carry.
