# Research Scope Register

## Purpose

This register separates discoveries by the question they answer rather than by
how striking they appear. It protects the present manuscript from indefinite
expansion while preserving broader work for a coherent second paper.

## Current Manuscript Boundary

The present paper concerns numerical relationships carried by divine names,
titles, complete divine formulas, and tightly bounded scriptural settings in
which those objects meet. A new finding enters only when it satisfies all of
the following:

1. it directly involves an admitted divine name, title, formula, or an exact
   lexical occurrence of one;
2. its textual unit exists before calculation and is not assembled from
   distant or selectively omitted words;
3. its arithmetic, basis, spelling, and source are reproducible;
4. a finite comparison set or an explicit exploratory limitation is recorded;
5. negative variants and competing examples remain visible;
6. the result advances the manuscript's existing thesis without requiring a
   new theory of book, chapter, or canon-level structure.

Two post-publication discoveries are authorized for the current manuscript
revision:

- The Psalm 22 case joins YHWH, Yeshua, and salvation inside recognized units
  at the Psalm's opening. Its integration source is
  `research/PSALM_22_EXPLORATION.md`.
- The base-12 repdigit ladder organizes admitted divine names, formulas, and
  exact components already central to the manuscript. Its complete eleven-rung
  universe, empty rungs, additive relations, and finite subset census are
  recorded in `research/REPDIGIT_LADDER_EXPLORATION.md`.

The ladder does not authorize arbitrary words chosen to fill a missing rung.
In particular, `66_12 = 78_10` remains unoccupied because the only matching
Psalm-opening window is not a name, title, component, or complete formula.
No other exploratory discovery is authorized for this revision.

## Next Paper Boundary

The next paper will study recognized biblical units larger than divine names.
Its working subject is base-12 and related numerical architecture among book
names, Psalm titles, canonical divisions, and declared combinations of those
units. Reserved material includes:

- values and palindromes of the individual books of the Tanakh;
- predeclared combinations such as the paired books of Kings;
- the conventional Tanakh total 929 and the 260 New Testament chapters;
- comparisons among Jewish and Christian book-grouping conventions;
- a uniform corpus of Psalm superscriptions and recognized title phrases;
- figurate, prime, square, and related mathematical structures attached to
  those declared units.

Before calculation, that paper must freeze its canonical conventions, spelling
registry, combination rules, and comparison families. It must not become a
collection of every pattern found anywhere in the Bible.

## Research Atlas

The private research layer retains observations that are interesting but not
ready for either argument:

- arbitrary word-window hits outside a declared textual question;
- tokenization-sensitive word, letter, verse, or chapter counts;
- unrestricted combinations of books, phrases, or values;
- mathematical-constant resemblances without an independently meaningful
  textual unit;
- low-priority offsets, variant spellings, and post-hoc conjunctions;
- leads awaiting source verification, matched controls, or a coherent thesis.

An atlas result may move into a paper only after its destination, textual unit,
search boundary, and negative tests are fixed in writing. Strength alone is
not enough; topical fit is required.
